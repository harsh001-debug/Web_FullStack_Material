**🧠 SCI-CHARADES: Structured Game Rules**

---

## 1. Game Overview

Sci-Charades is an interactive science-themed acting and guessing game.
Participants act out scientific terms without speaking, while their teammate attempts to guess the correct word within a limited time.

Each team has two members:

* **Actor** – acts out the scientific term using gestures and movements.
* **Guesser** – attempts to identify the correct scientific word.

Scientific terms are written on **folded pieces of paper placed inside a bowl**.
At the start of a turn, the actor **randomly picks one folded paper from the bowl** to receive the word.

The game progresses through **multiple rounds with increasing difficulty**.

---

# 2. Team Formation

## 2.1 Participant Pairing

* Individual participants will be **paired randomly** before the game starts.
* Each pair forms **one team**.

## 2.2 Team Roles

Each team will have two roles:

**Actor**

* Receives the scientific word.
* Performs it using gestures and body movements.

**Guesser**

* Observes the acting.
* Attempts to guess the correct word.

## 2.3 Handling Odd Participants

If the number of participants is:

**Even**

* Teams form normally.

**Odd**

* One participant may temporarily sit out, or
* The participant may join once another participant becomes available.

---

# 3. Game Flow

## 3.1 One-to-One Match

Teams compete in **1 vs 1 matches** against another team.

Each team gets a **separate turn** to perform and guess.

---

## 3.2 Steps in a Round

For each match:

1. One team sends their **actor**.
2. The actor **picks one folded paper from the bowl**.
3. The actor reads the scientific word silently.
4. The actor performs the word using **actions only**.
5. The guesser tries to identify the word.

---

# 4. Time Rules

* Each team receives **120 seconds (2 minutes)**.
* Within this time:

  * The actor performs the word.
  * The guesser attempts to guess the word.

The **time taken to correctly guess the word will be recorded**.

---

# 5. Difficulty Levels

The difficulty of words increases in each round.

| Round   | Difficulty |
| ------- | ---------- |
| Round 1 | Easy       |
| Round 2 | Medium     |
| Round 3 | Difficult  |

---

# 6. Round Selection Logic (Threshold Rule)

To efficiently manage a large number of teams, a **threshold elimination rule** is used.

## 6.1 Threshold Calculation

[
Threshold = Ceiling(\frac{Number\ of\ Teams}{2})
]

This determines the **maximum number of teams that can move to the next round**.

---

### Example 1

If there are **50 teams**

Threshold = ceil(50 ÷ 2)
Threshold = **25 teams**

➡ Only **25 teams advance** to the next round.

---

### Example 2

If there are **17 teams**

Threshold = ceil(17 ÷ 2)
Threshold = **9 teams**

* If **more than 9 teams win**, only the **top 9 fastest teams** advance.
* If **9 or fewer teams win**, **all winners advance**.

---

# 7. Winner Sorting Rule

At the end of **every round**, all winning teams will be **sorted in ascending order of the guessing time**.

This means:

**Lower time = higher ranking**

This ranking will be used to determine which teams move forward if the **number of winners exceeds the threshold**.

---

# 8. Final Round Selection

In the **3rd (final) round**:

* If **more than 3 teams qualify**,
* The **Top 3 teams with the fastest guessing time** will be declared winners.

---

# 9. Scoring / Advancement Criteria

Teams advance based on:

1. **Correct Guess**
2. **Time Taken**

Priority rule:

1️⃣ Correct guess
2️⃣ Faster guessing time (tie-breaker)

---

# 10. Acting Rules

The actor must follow these rules.

### Allowed

* Body movements
* Gestures
* Acting and miming

### Not Allowed

* Speaking words
* Saying letters
* Writing the word
* Lip-syncing the word
* Spelling the word through gestures

---

# 11. Fair Play Policy

Any form of cheating or misconduct is strictly prohibited.

### Examples of Cheating

* Conveying letters of the word
* Whispering hints
* Using phones or objects
* Communicating with the audience

### Consequences

➡ Immediate **team disqualification**.

---

# 12. Organizer Authority

The **organizing team’s decision is final**.

Participants must:

* Follow all rules
* Respect organizers
* Maintain proper sportsmanship and decorum.
